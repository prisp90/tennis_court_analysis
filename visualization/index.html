Assessement

This assessement is designed to provide insightful information into the supply-demand ratio of public tennis courts, which are important assets for the public health. The information will be valuable for informing decisions related to the development of tennis court facilities in areas with potential higher demand or availability.

Higher supply-demand ratios show that the number of existing public tennis courts exceeds the number of demanded public tennis courts in those areas. The number of demanded courts is analyzed by a statistical model based on the population and trend of tennis players. Conversely, a lower supply-demand ratio suggests a need for increased supply to meet the demand for tennis courts.

In this project, we aim the chicago metropolitan area as a case study of the assessment. The limitations of this project include the exclusion of certain cities due to insufficient data on population, city boundaries, and tennis court availability.

Analysis Summary

Out of a total of 215 targeted cities, we assess 185 cities. Among the 185 cities, 172 cities have a supply-demand ratio of less than 1, the average supply-demand ratio is 0.449 and 9 cities do not have any tennis courts, suggesting a potential demand for more public tennis courts to meet the needs of the community.

The total number of tennis players in the Chicago metropolitan area serves as the foundation for determining the demand for tennis court facilities, with a count of 618902 players.

Education, wealth, age, and ethnicity are key factors that affect the number of tennis players and, subsequently, the demand for tennis courts.
Approximately 60.1% of the Chicago metropolitan area tennis players were between the ages of 18 to 65.
68.5% of tennis players in the Chicago metropolitan area identify as white, which is the largest ethnicity group.
Compared to the Chicago metropolitan area population, a Chicago metropolitan area tennis player was more likely to have an income of $92,338 or more
39.9% tennis players have college degree or higher.
